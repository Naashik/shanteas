Types of Teas

We supply fermented, flavorful black Ceylon tea. This tea type represents the iconic body, strength, color, and pungency of the fine Sri Lankan tea. To get the best out of our black Ceylon tea, brew it for at least three minutes in spring water.
Tea enthusiasts that enjoy extra-strong flavors can brew it for 4-5 minutes and complement it with warm, dairy milk- if needed.

Shan Teas (Private) Limited also provides an assortment of regional teas grown in the various regions of Sri Lanka. Ceylon has seven agro-climatic regions, each producing its unique flavors and variations of traditional Ceylon tea. The regional teas are grown in:

  • Low County - In regions of Ruhuna and Sabaragamuwa
  • Mid Country - In the Kandy region
  • Hill Country - In regions of Nuwara Eliya, Dambulla, Uva & Uda Pussellawa

Regional Teas

Silver tips (White tea) is one of the rarest and most refined forms of tea for discerning tea connoisseurs. This type of tea is created from the hand-picked buds of the Camellia Sinensis plant. The manufacturing process for this tea is also quite different. The buds are hand-picked and sun-dried using a meticulous approach.

Silver Tips

Golden tips are also refined teas that speak to the taste buds of all tea connoisseurs. They are also extracted and handpicked from the Camellia Sinensis plant. The manufacturing process is quite distinct. The buds are handpicked, sprayed with black tea liquid, and sun-dried.

Golden Tips
